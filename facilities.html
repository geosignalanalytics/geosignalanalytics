Facilities

Advanced computational, analytical, and collaborative infrastructure supporting cutting-edge geophysical research and innovation.

Research Infrastructure

The GeoSignal Research Institute maintains a modern research environment designed to support high-level scientific investigation in geophysics, signal processing, and data-driven subsurface analysis.

Our facilities emphasize computational efficiency, methodological rigor, and collaborative research workflows rather than traditional laboratory constraints, enabling flexible and scalable scientific development.

Computational Facilities

  • High-Performance Computing (HPC)

    Scalable computing resources for large-scale seismic processing, numerical simulations, inversion, and deep learning model training.

  • AI & Data Science Environment

    Dedicated software stacks for machine learning, physics-informed modeling, signal processing, and big geophysical data analytics.

  • Remote & Cloud-Based Infrastructure

    Secure access to cloud platforms and remote servers enabling collaborative research and distributed computational workflows.

Geophysical Data Resources

  • Seismic & DAS Data Archives

    Curated datasets including seismic waveforms, Distributed Acoustic Sensing (DAS) records, and synthetic benchmark models.

  • Near-Surface & Subsurface Data

    Electrical resistivity, seismic refraction, magnetometry, gravimetry, and induced polarization datasets for applied research.

  • Environmental & Hazard Monitoring Data

    Data resources supporting seismic risk assessment, environmental monitoring, and infrastructure safety studies.

Collaborative & Training Spaces

In addition to computational facilities, the Institute provides virtual and hybrid collaborative environments supporting seminars, workshops, training programs, and international research meetings.

These spaces are designed to foster interdisciplinary exchange, mentoring, and scientific dialogue across academia, industry, and public institutions.
