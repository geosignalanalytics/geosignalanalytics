About GeoSignal Analytics LLC

GeoSignal Analytics LLC is a leading geophysical data analytics firm specializing in advanced signal processing, machine learning, and innovative geoscience research. Founded by Dr. Innocent Oboué, the company bridges the gap between academia and industry by providing cutting-edge solutions for geophysical data analysis.

We leverage a multidisciplinary approach, combining expertise in geophysics, data science, and machine learning to solve complex subsurface imaging problems. Our goal is to transform raw geophysical data into actionable insights that support energy exploration, environmental monitoring, and infrastructure development.

With a commitment to scientific excellence, collaboration, and innovation, GeoSignal Analytics LLC is dedicated to driving research forward and contributing to advancements in the field of geophysics.

GeoSignal Research Institute

The GeoSignal Research Institute is the academic and scientific branch of GeoSignal Analytics LLC, dedicated to applied research, computational modeling, geophysical data analysis, and scientific publication in the fields of geosciences and artificial intelligence. The institute aims to advance scientific understanding, foster innovation in subsurface imaging, and build strong partnerships with universities, government agencies, and private companies.

Key Focus Areas

1. Signal Processing & Denoising

Development of advanced filtering and denoising algorithms for seismic and Distributed Acoustic Sensing (DAS) data to enhance data quality and resolution.

2. Machine Learning for Geosciences

Integration of data-driven models, neural networks, and physics-guided AI approaches for subsurface characterization and imaging.

3. Ambient Noise & Interferometry

Extraction of empirical Green’s functions and tomography using passive seismic data for monitoring environmental and structural changes.

4. Computational Geophysics

Numerical simulation, inverse modeling, and high-performance computing for seismic wave propagation and geophysical model reconstruction.

5. Open Science & Collaboration

Promotion of transparent, reproducible, and collaborative research through open-source software, data sharing, and interdisciplinary partnerships.
