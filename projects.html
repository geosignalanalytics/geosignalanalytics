Research Projects

Ongoing and emerging research initiatives addressing critical challenges in subsurface imaging, geophysical monitoring, and Earth system understanding.

Ongoing Research Projects

AI-Driven Seismic and DAS Denoising

This project focuses on the development of physics-informed and data-driven algorithms for seismic and Distributed Acoustic Sensing (DAS) noise attenuation. Advanced deep learning architectures are combined with classical signal processing to enhance weak seismic phases and improve subsurface interpretability.

Subsurface Imaging for Groundwater and Environmental Applications

The project aims to improve near-surface and shallow subsurface imaging using electrical resistivity tomography, seismic refraction, and integrated geophysical inversion. Applications include groundwater exploration, contamination assessment, and environmental monitoring.

Mineral and Geothermal Resource Characterization

This research investigates the use of magnetometry, gravimetry, induced polarization, and spectral analysis to characterize mineral deposits and geothermal systems. Emphasis is placed on integrated interpretation and uncertainty-aware modeling.

Emerging and Conceptual Projects

Deep Earth Imaging and Mantle Structure Analysis

This emerging project explores advanced seismic imaging techniques for probing the deep Earth, including mantle transition zone discontinuities and core–mantle boundary structures using global and regional seismic data.

Seismic Monitoring and Hazard Forecasting

The objective of this project is to develop robust monitoring and forecasting frameworks for seismic hazards, integrating real-time seismic observations, statistical modeling, and machine learning approaches for risk mitigation.

Integrated Subsurface Intelligence for Energy Transition

This initiative targets the development of multi-physics and multi-scale subsurface models supporting geothermal energy, carbon storage, and sustainable hydrocarbon production in the context of global energy transition.
