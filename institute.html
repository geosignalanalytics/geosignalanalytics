A professional and research-oriented training by the GeoSignal Research Institute

Explore Modules

Course Overview

The Signal Processing for Geoscientists and Engineers course bridges theory and practice by exploring modern approaches to data analysis, denoising, and reconstruction in geophysical applications. Participants gain hands-on experience with both traditional algorithms and deep learning architectures designed for seismic and Distributed Acoustic Sensing (DAS) data.

Special emphasis is placed on simultaneous denoising and reconstruction of 2D–5D seismic datasets using state-of-the-art frameworks such as Deep Image Prior (DIP), Two-Step DIP (TSDIP), and transformer-based networks.

Course Modules

1. Introduction to Signal Processing Concepts

Sampling, aliasing, signal-to-noise ratio (SNR), and digital filtering fundamentals with applications to geophysical signals.

2. Spectral and Time–Frequency Analysis

Fourier, STFT, and Hilbert–Huang methods for non-stationary signal representation and interpretation.

3. Advanced Denoising Techniques

Implementation of Non-Local Means (NLM), Deep Image Prior (DIP), and Two-Step DIP (TSDIP) approaches for seismic and DAS data.

4. Simultaneous Denoising and Reconstruction of 2D–5D Data

Integration of traditional rank-reduction, curvelet, and F–X prediction methods with modern deep learning models for multidimensional data recovery.

5. Machine Learning for Signal Processing

Application of ML regression, classification, and unsupervised methods for signal segmentation and feature enhancement.

6. Hands-on Case Studies

Full workflow execution from data preprocessing to reconstruction and visualization using MATLAB and Python.

7. Capstone Project & Certification

Participants complete an independent project and receive certification from the GeoSignal Research Institute (GSRI).

Who Should Attend

This course is designed for graduate students, early-career researchers, and industry professionals working in geophysics, civil and environmental engineering, or data science who want to integrate AI into signal processing workflows.
