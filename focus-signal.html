Signal Processing for Geoscientists and Engineers

Instructor: Dr. [Your Name], GeoSignal Research Institute (GSRI)

Format: Hybrid (Online + Hands-on Workshop)

Level: Intermediate to Advanced

Duration: 4–6 weeks (customizable for professionals)

Course Overview

This course provides a comprehensive introduction to modern signal processing and data reconstruction techniques used in geoscience. Participants will explore advanced theories and practical applications ranging from classical filtering to deep learning–based seismic data enhancement. Special emphasis is placed on simultaneous denoising and reconstruction of high-dimensional seismic data (2D–5D) using both traditional signal processing and deep learning frameworks such as DIP, TSDIP, and transformer-inspired networks.

Learning Outcomes

  • Understand and model various types of geophysical noise.
  • Apply Fourier, wavelet, curvelet, and sparse transform methods for feature extraction.
  • Perform simultaneous denoising and reconstruction of seismic datasets in 2D–5D.
  • Integrate deep learning architectures (DIP, CNN, transformer-based) into seismic workflows.
  • Analyze data in the frequency–wavenumber and time–frequency domains.
  • Automate workflows in MATLAB and Python for seismic and DAS datasets.

Course Modules

  1. Introduction to Signal Processing Concepts
    • Sampling, aliasing, and signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) fundamentals.
    • Digital filtering and noise modeling for geophysical signals.
  2. Spectral and Time–Frequency Analysis
    • Fourier, Short-Time Fourier Transform (STFT), and Hilbert–Huang methods.
    • Adaptive time–frequency representations for non-stationary signals.
  3. Advanced Denoising Techniques
    • Non-Local Means (NLM), Deep Image Prior (DIP), and Two-Step DIP (TSDIP).
    • Patch-based and iterative learning strategies for noise suppression.
  4. Simultaneous Denoising and Reconstruction of Seismic Data (2D–5D)
    • Traditional methods: Rank reduction, curvelet transform, and F–X prediction.
    • Deep learning methods: DIP/TSDIP architectures, attention-based models, and hybrid loss design.
    • Case studies on real 3D/4D seismic volumes and distributed acoustic sensing (DAS) datasets.
  5. Machine Learning for Signal Processing
    • Regression, classification, and unsupervised feature learning for signal segmentation.
    • Integrating ML pipelines for noise and event separation.
  6. Hands-on Case Studies
    • Full data workflow: preprocessing → denoising → reconstruction → visualization.
    • Comparison of traditional vs. deep learning methods on seismic and DAS data.
  7. Capstone Project & Certification
    • Independent project using participant data.
    • Peer presentation and certification by the GeoSignal Research Institute (GSRI).

Who Should Attend

  • Graduate students and researchers in Geophysics, Civil/Environmental Engineering, or Applied Physics.
  • Data scientists interested in applying AI to geophysical signal processing.
  • Industry professionals in energy exploration, environmental monitoring, or seismic imaging.
