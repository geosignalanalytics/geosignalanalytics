DAS Data Analysis & Interpretation

Advanced training program dedicated to the processing, analysis, and interpretation of Distributed Acoustic Sensing (DAS) data for subsurface monitoring and seismic applications.

Program Introduction

This program provides a comprehensive introduction to DAS data analysis, focusing on both theoretical foundations and practical workflows used in research and field deployments.

Program Scope

Participants will explore DAS acquisition principles, unique signal characteristics, noise sources, and advanced processing strategies tailored to large-scale sensing systems.

Program Topics

  • DAS acquisition principles and system configurations
  • DAS signal characteristics and noise mechanisms
  • Preprocessing, filtering, and denoising techniques
  • Seismic and ambient noise applications
  • Case studies from real DAS deployments

Courses / Teaching Materials

  • 1. Fundamentals of Distributed Acoustic Sensing
    Physical principles of DAS, sensing mechanisms, spatial sampling, gauge length effects, and comparison with conventional sensors.
  • 2. DAS Signal Processing and Noise Mitigation
    Signal conditioning, filtering strategies, denoising approaches, and handling DAS-specific artifacts.
  • 3. Seismic and Ambient Noise Applications with DAS
    Earthquake monitoring, surface wave analysis, interferometry, and passive imaging using DAS data.
  • 4. Advanced Interpretation and Case Studies
    Interpretation workflows, uncertainty considerations, and real-world DAS applications in research and industry.

Target Audience

Researchers, engineers, and practitioners working with DAS, seismic monitoring systems, and large-scale sensing technologies.
