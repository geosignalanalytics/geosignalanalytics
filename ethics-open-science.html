Ethics & Open Science

Upholding scientific integrity, transparency, and responsible research practices in geophysical and computational Earth sciences.

Scientific Integrity and Research Ethics

The GeoSignal Research Institute (GSRI) is committed to the highest standards of scientific integrity, ethical conduct, and professional responsibility. All research activities conducted within the Institute adhere to internationally recognized principles of honesty, rigor, transparency, and accountability.

GSRI promotes ethical research practices across data acquisition, processing, interpretation, and publication, ensuring that scientific outcomes are reproducible, verifiable, and free from conflicts of interest.

Responsible Use of Data

The Institute recognizes the critical importance of responsible data stewardship, particularly in geophysical research involving sensitive environmental, infrastructural, or societal contexts.

GSRI follows best practices in data governance, including appropriate data anonymization, secure storage, and compliance with legal, institutional, and contractual obligations associated with proprietary or confidential datasets.

Open Science and Reproducibility

GSRI actively supports the principles of Open Science by encouraging transparent methodologies, reproducible workflows, and, where possible, open dissemination of research outputs.

This includes the publication of open-access articles, the sharing of codes, algorithms, and computational frameworks, and the documentation of data-processing pipelines to facilitate independent verification and reuse by the scientific community.

Artificial Intelligence and Ethical AI

As a research institute working at the intersection of geophysics and artificial intelligence, GSRI places particular emphasis on the ethical development and application of AI-based methods.

We prioritize interpretable, physics-informed, and scientifically grounded machine learning approaches, avoiding black-box solutions when they compromise scientific understanding, robustness, or accountability.

Publication Ethics and Authorship

GSRI adheres to internationally accepted standards regarding authorship, citation practices, peer review, and publication ethics. All contributors to scientific work are appropriately acknowledged, and plagiarism, data fabrication, and duplicate publication are strictly prohibited.

The Institute encourages responsible peer-review participation and constructive scientific dialogue as essential components of academic integrity.

Societal Responsibility

Beyond academic excellence, GSRI acknowledges its responsibility toward society. Research activities are conducted with careful consideration of their environmental, social, and ethical implications.

By promoting ethical science and open knowledge, the Institute seeks to contribute to informed decision-making, sustainable development, and the responsible use of subsurface resources worldwide.
